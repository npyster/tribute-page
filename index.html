Kobe Bryant

One of the greatest basketball players to have ever played the game

Kobe Bryant's History

Professional Career

  • June 26, 1996 - Drafted No. 13th overall by the Charlotte Hornet's - Traded to the Lakers shortly after.
  • 1996 - 1997 - Bryant becomes the youngest player in NBA history as he begins his pro career at 18 years, 2 months and 11 days. He is named to the NBA All-Rookie second team after averaging 7.6 points in 15.5 minutes per game off the bench.
  • February 1997 - He wins the All-Star Weekend slam dunk competition, but he does not play in the All-Star Game.
  • 1998 - At 19, he becomes the youngest starter ever in his first NBA All-Star Game.
  • 1999 - 2000 - Wins first NBA Title
  • 2000 - 01 - Wins second NBA Title
  • Feb. 2002 - Named All-Star Game MVP for the first time. He also won the honor in 2007, 2009 and 2011.
  • 2001 - 02Wins third consecutive NBA Title
  • Jan. 22, 2006 - Bryant scores 81 points in the Lakers’ 122-104 victory over Toronto. Only Wilt Chamberlain’s 100-point game in 1962 tops this performance. Wins Scoring Title for that season.
  • 2006 - 07 - Wins another scoring title
  • Aug. 24, 2008 - Bryant wins a gold medal at the Beijing Olympics as a member of the U.S. men's basketball team.
  • June 14, 2009 - Wins fourth NBA Title and is named Finals MVP
  • Feb. 1, 2010 - Kobe surpasses Jerry West's franchise record of 25,192 points scored as a Laker.
  • June 17, 2010 - Wins fifth NBA Title and is named Finals MVP
  • Aug. 12, 2012 - Bryant wins his second Olympic gold medal as a member of the U.S. men’s basketball team at the 2012 Games in London.
  • 2012 - 13 - Kobe passes Wilt Chamberlain for fourth on the NBA's all-time scoring list.
  • Dec. 14, 2014 - Kobe passes Michael Jordan to become third on the NBA's all-time scoring list.
  • April 13, 2016 - Kobe plays his last game and retires after 20 seasons with the Los Angeles Lakers.
